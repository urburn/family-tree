McGinley Coats of Arms

A survey of heraldic traditions associated with the McGinley name

MagFhionnghaile · County Donegal · Ireland

Unlike the centralised heraldic systems of England or Scotland, Irish clan identity was expressed through banners and battle standards long before the Norman concept of the shield-borne coat of arms arrived. The McGinley name — MagFhionnghaile in Irish, "of the fair-valoured" — is ancient to County Donegal. Several distinct heraldic traditions have been recorded in connection with the name; three are presented here.

A note on Irish heraldry: The McGinley Clan website notes that the authentic Irish fighting tradition used banners and flags (Bratach) as clan identifiers — not the decorated shield, which was a Norman innovation later adopted by the English and eventually applied to Irish surnames by outside observers. What we call the "McGinley coat of arms" is therefore more accurately a clan emblem adapted to heraldic convention by later commentators. Unlike English heraldry, where arms are granted to specific individuals and strictly inherited, Irish clan heraldry developed loosely — arms associated with a surname were used by many branches of a clan, and different sources record different versions. The variants below should be understood in that spirit.

The McGinley Clan

See also: mcginleyclan.org →
Field: Or (gold).
Design: A red cross crosslet fitchy (lower arm tapering to a point), with a black roundel at the tip of each crosslet arm. Similar to the O'Donnell arms as depicted on John Goghe's Map of Ireland (1567). The three black dots represent the three saints most closely associated with the McGinley clan. The red cross may derive from the McDonald clan of Scotland — with whom the Donegal leadership had strong ties — or, per medieval folklore, from St. Patrick's gift of the red cross symbol to Conall Gulban, ancestor of the Donegal clans.
Crest: An Irish harp Or.
Clan War Cry (in lieu of motto)
Clann Mhig Fhionnghaile Abú
"Clan McGinley forever"
Source: The McGinley Clan — Coat of Arms page

Field: Argent (silver/white) — denotes Peace and Sincerity.
Charges: A bend Sable (black diagonal band) — a bearing of High Honour, signifying Defence or Protection — charged with three escallops (scallop shells) Argent, symbolising the Protection of Providence.
Crest: A helm with gold and green mantling.
Motto
None recorded
No motto is listed in this source.

McGinley Family Arms

See also: mcginley.online →
Blazon: Per pale Or and Azure, a torch Argent enflamed Or overall; in dexter chief a cross Sable; in sinister chief an open book Argent.
Symbolism: The shield is divided per pale (vertically) into gold and blue. A cross on the gold field; an open book on the blue field. A torch at the centre spans both halves, overall.
Motto — Latin
Fides Dividit, Ratio Unit
"Faith divides, Reason unites"
Motto — Irish Gaelic (alternative rendering)
Scarann Creideamh, Aontaíonn Réasún
"Faith divides, Reason unites"
Source: mcginley.online
