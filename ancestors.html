🍀 The McGinley Line — Ireland to England to Canada

The McGinley family traces from Dundork, near Dublin, Ireland, through Lancashire, England, and on to Vancouver, Canada by the 1920s.

Maurice McGinley was born at Dundork, near Dublin, Ireland, and at some point moved his family to Lancashire, England — part of the large Irish migration to the industrial north of England during the 19th century. He is the earliest known ancestor of the Canadian McGinley line.

Francis McGinley, son of Maurice McGinley, married Mary Agnes Wynne — known in the family as "Marian" — on 4th September 1901 at St. Marie's Roman Catholic Church in Widnes, Lancashire. The ceremony was performed by Father O'Brien. Together they had seven children, of whom baby Maurice appears in the family photographs.

Maurice McGinley was born on 30th November 1902 in Widnes, Lancashire, England — the same parish where his parents had married just the year before. He worked as an accountant and eventually settled in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada.

On 16th August 1927, he married Henrietta Teresa Tall at Our Lady of Perpetual Help parish in Vancouver. Their union brought together the McGinley line from Ireland and England with the Tall–Stephen line from Scotland. They had two sons: Francis George (born 25 April 1929) and Maurice Gerrard, known as Gerry (born 19 September 1931).

Henrietta Teresa Tall was born on 29 April 1900 in Lerwick, the capital of the Shetland Islands, Scotland. Her father William Tall was a Royal Navy sailor from Lancaster, England; her mother was Henrietta (Ettie) Stephen, daughter of the prominent Peterhead shipbuilding family. The family eventually settled in Vancouver.

She married Maurice McGinley on 16 August 1927 and together they raised two sons in Vancouver. She lived to 1990, reaching the age of 90.

⚓ The Stephen Line — Peterhead, Scotland

The Stephens were shipbuilders, fish curers, and seafarers of Peterhead. Their ancestry reaches back to the Napoleonic Wars.

Great Great Great Grandfather

George Stephen

Trafalgar Veteran, 1805
Notable
Battle of Trafalgar, 21 Oct 1805
Served with
Lord Nelson, Royal Navy

George Stephen served in the Royal Navy and was present at the Battle of Trafalgar on 21 October 1805 — one of history's most decisive naval engagements, in which Admiral Lord Nelson defeated the combined French and Spanish fleets, though Nelson himself was killed. Like Nelson, George Stephen lost an eye in the battle.

He never tired of recounting the qualities of his hero, and family heirlooms from his life — including a mahogany table and a waistcoat of brocade satin — were still in the possession of a relative at the time these records were compiled.

Robert Stephen was born in Boddam, Scotland, near Peterhead, the son of James Stephen and Ann Cordiner. He married Alicia Margaret West — known as Alice — in the parish of Cookney, Muchalls, on 6 April 1868. Together they had twelve children.

In 1902, Robert emigrated to the Northwest Territories of Canada, becoming a true pioneer. He built the Bar S Ranch, worked as a boat builder, cabinet-maker, and ferry-man, and was noted as a fish curer and inventor. Alice died in 1917 in Vancouver. Robert lived until 1928, dying at age 86.

Their children included: Alice, Susan Ann (Mrs. D.D.C. Murray, M.B.E.), Henrietta (who married William Tall), James Garden, William Andrew (died in infancy), Agnes Jane, Thomas Hutchinson, George Craib, Elsie Gordon, Sarah Elizabeth, and John Clark.

⚔️ The West Line — Ireland, Scotland & the Royal Navy

The West family, through Alice West who married Robert Stephen in 1868, traces back to a distinguished naval lineage including Vice Admiral Temple West, commemorated in Westminster Abbey.

Distant Ancestor

Vice Admiral Temple West

Died: 1757, age 43 · Commemorated in Westminster Abbey, North Aisle
Entered Navy
September 1727, aboard HMS Revenge
Father
Richard West, D.D., prebendary of Winchester
Spouse
Daughter of Admiral Sir John Balchen
Notable
Battle of the Second Cape Finisterre, May 1747; Battle of Minorca, May 1756

Temple West entered the Royal Navy in September 1727 as a volunteer aboard HMS Revenge, the beginning of a distinguished career that would take him to the rank of Vice Admiral of the White. He was the son of Richard West, D.D., prebendary of Winchester, by his wife Maria, eldest daughter of Sir Richard Remple (1634–1697).

He married the daughter of Admiral Sir John Balchen. In the signal victory over the French fleet on 3 May 1747 (the Second Battle of Cape Finisterre), he was Captain of the ship carrying Sir Peter Warren, and distinguished himself brilliantly. At the Battle of Minorca on 20 May 1756, as Rear Admiral commanding the second division, his courage was admired by the whole British squadron.

On 17 November 1757, he was appointed one of the Lord's Commissioners of the Admiralty. He died the same year, at the age of 43. His monument in the North Aisle of Westminster Abbey was erected by his widow — the daughter of the brave Admiral Balchen — to preserve to posterity his fame and example. His brother was Gilbert West, the author (1703–1756). Jane West, the authoress, was also a relative.

Great Great Grandmother

Susan Brown West

Home

Susan Brown came from Clifden, Co. Galway, Ireland — her family were titled people who opposed her marriage to Garden West of Kingtown (now Dún Laoghaire), near Dublin, as he was considered not of their station. It was her brother who introduced the couple, having become great friends with Garden through their shared love of violin music while serving in the Navy together.

Susan's parents did not attend the wedding, and she never returned home — though her mother did send her personal maid to be with Susan. Lady Alice Fisher of Terrehaute attended the wedding and corresponded with Susan Ann Stephen (Mrs. D.D.C. Murray) about the occasion.

Their daughter Alicia (Alice) Margaret West was born in 1846 in Portsoy, Scotland, and went on to marry Robert Stephen in 1868. An Admiral of Napoleon's era from the Brown family served in the West Indies.

🌍 Connected Relatives

Notable relatives connected to the family through the Stephen and West lines.

Great Aunt · Stephen Line

Susan Ann Stephen (Mrs. D.D.C. Murray, M.B.E.)

Born 1872 · Died 1960, South Africa

Susan Ann was the second eldest daughter of Robert and Alice Stephen of Peterhead. During the First World War she established numerous Red Cross stations throughout South Africa, for which she was presented at Court and honoured by the Queen with the M.B.E.

Her husband, Donald D.C. Murray, D.S.O., was a prominent businessman and statesman. Their home "Haldon," outside Bloemfontein, South Africa, occupied 125 acres and was celebrated for its hospitality — welcoming dignitaries including General Lord Haig and Mrs. Haig.

Connected Relative · Murray

D.D.C. Murray, D.S.O.

Bloemfontein, South Africa

Donald D.C. Murray was born and educated at Castetown, Caithness, Scotland. He worked for the Commercial Bank of Scotland before joining the National Bank of South Africa. On the invitation of Sir John Fraser, he became a partner in Fraser & Scott, a large commercial and real estate firm in Bloemfontein.

He served with the South African Forces in the First World War and was awarded the D.S.O., becoming Honorary Colonel of Bloemfontein. He was elected to the Senate and became Chief Whip of the United Party, a close friend of General Smuts. He coordinated the arrangements for the Royal visit to Bloemfontein (the King, Queen, and Princesses) and was Chief of the Caledonian Society of South Africa for many years.

Great Uncle · Tall Line

John Tall

England

John Tall was the only brother of William Tall (Great Grandfather), and the elder son of Ayling Tall. He married Clara Symes, his first cousin — the daughter of Ayling Tall's sister. Their children were Stella, Ethel (neither married), and Sylvia, who married Ted Morey and lived on the Isle of Wight with their four children.

Great Uncle · Tall Line

William Stephen Tall

Born 1897, Peterhead · Died 1978, California, U.S.A.

William Stephen Tall was the son of William Tall and Henrietta Stephen, and brother of Grandmother Henrietta Teresa. He became an aircraft mechanic and settled in California, U.S.A. He married twice (and divorced). He died in 1978 in California.

Family Origins Summary: The four lines that converged in the McGinley family of Vancouver came from: the Irish midlands (McGinley — Dundork near Dublin); coastal Scotland (Stephen — Peterhead and Boddam); northern England and Scotland (Tall — Lancaster, Lytham, and Lerwick in the Shetlands); and Ireland and Scotland (West — Clifden, Galway and Portsoy, Scotland). By 1927, all lines had made their way to Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada.
